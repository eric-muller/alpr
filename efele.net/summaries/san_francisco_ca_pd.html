San Francisco Police Department ALPR usage

October 13, 2025

Table of contents

 

This report is based on data obtained via California Public Record Act request P222366-090425, for data related to the ALPR system operated by the San Francisco Police Department (hereafter SFPD). It covers the period August 16, 2024 through June 2, 2025.

The SFPD decided to redact the case number and reason from the Organization and Network audits. Consequently, there is not enough data to reconstruct cases (even approximately), and each query is treated as a separate case.

 

The Transparency portal.

1:1 Sharing audit

Under 1:1 Sharing, each agency decides which other agencies can access its data. Agency A can share with agency B without B sharing with A.

SFPD and X allow reciprocal searches: 222 agencies.

SFPD allows searches, but X does not: 57 agencies.

SFPD does not allow searches but X does: 37 agencies.

Given the number of non CA agencies, it seems likely that their queries were enabled by National Lookup, rather than by 1:1 Sharing (which would have required to select all those agencies one by one, or selecting agencies by a very large radius).

These queries are clear violations of California SB 40, as California state and local law enforcement agencies have been reminded by the California Attorney General in 2023.

Sharing via Statewide lookup

SFPD participates in Statewide lookup. This allows California agencies to search the SFPD data without any prior agreement on sharing, but limits the searches to full license plates.

54 agencies without 1:1 Sharing have accessed the SFPD data since February 12, 2025.

As we have seen in the previous section, the queries by other agencies dominate. From the point of view of those agencies, SFPD is just one of many agencies, more or less geographically distant. When SFPD’s data is searched, there are probably many other agencies searched as well, and therefore many cameras involved, hence the high proportion of broad searches.
